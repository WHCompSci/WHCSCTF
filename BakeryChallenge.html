What We Bake

Sourdough Loaves

Our long-fermented country sourdough has a crackly crust and open, chewy crumb. Each loaf is baked in a cast iron combo cooker for maximum oven spring.

🥐
Butter Croissants

Made with imported French butter and folded over three days. Flaky, honeycomb layers that shatter at first bite. Available plain, almond, or chocolate.

Thick, pillowy focaccia dimpled with olive oil, sea salt, and whatever seasonal toppings our bakers feel inspired by that morning.

Our Story

A tiny kitchen.
A big obsession.

Flour & Stone started in 2009 as a pop-up at the Sunday farmers market. Our founder, Ada Carver, had spent three years apprenticing at a boulangerie in Lyon and came home with a sourdough starter that has never once been refrigerated.

We moved into our current space on Mercer Street in 2014. The stone deck oven was salvaged from a shuttered bakery in Vermont and took six weeks and a small miracle to install. It runs at 520°F and we haven't touched the dial since.

Everything is mixed by hand, shaped by hand, and baked to order. We don't use preservatives, dough conditioners, or shortcuts. We just use time.
